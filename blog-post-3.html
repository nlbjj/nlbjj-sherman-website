The Benefits of Private Lessons in BJJ

Private lessons can accelerate your progress significantly. Learn when to consider one-on-one training and how to make the most of your private sessions with our experienced instructors.

While group classes are the foundation of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu training, private lessons offer a unique opportunity to accelerate your learning and address specific areas of your game with personalized attention.

What Are Private Lessons?

Private lessons are one-on-one training sessions with an experienced instructor, tailored specifically to your needs, goals, and current skill level. These sessions provide undivided attention and customized instruction that's impossible to achieve in a group setting.

Key Benefits of Private Training

Private lessons offer several advantages that can significantly enhance your BJJ journey:

  • Personalized instruction: Focus on your specific weaknesses and goals
  • Faster progress: Accelerated learning through targeted training
  • Flexible scheduling: Train at times that work with your schedule
  • Individual attention: No waiting for your turn or competing for instructor time
  • Customized curriculum: Learn techniques that match your body type and style

When to Consider Private Lessons

Private lessons can be beneficial at any stage of your BJJ journey, but they're particularly valuable in these situations:

For Beginners

New students can benefit greatly from private instruction to learn proper fundamentals and avoid developing bad habits early on.

For Competition Preparation

If you're preparing for a tournament, private lessons can help you develop a specific game plan and work on competition-specific techniques.

For Overcoming Plateaus

When you feel stuck in your progress, private instruction can help identify and address specific areas that need improvement.

For Injury Recovery

Private lessons can help you modify techniques to work around injuries and gradually return to full training.

What to Expect in a Private Lesson

Each private lesson is customized to your needs, but here's what you can typically expect:

  1. Assessment: Your instructor will evaluate your current skill level and identify areas for improvement
  2. Goal setting: Together, you'll establish specific objectives for the session and long-term goals
  3. Technique instruction: Learn new techniques or refine existing ones with detailed explanations
  4. Drilling: Practice techniques with your instructor providing immediate feedback
  5. Rolling: Controlled sparring to apply what you've learned
  6. Review and homework: Discussion of what to work on between sessions

Making the Most of Your Private Sessions

To maximize the value of your private lessons, consider these tips:

Come Prepared

Arrive with specific questions or areas you'd like to work on. The more specific you can be about your goals, the better your instructor can help you.

Take Notes

Bring a notebook to record key points, techniques, and drills. This will help you remember and practice what you've learned.

Be Open to Feedback

Private lessons provide immediate, personalized feedback. Be receptive to corrections and suggestions for improvement.

Practice Between Sessions

Work on the techniques and concepts you've learned in your private lessons during your regular training sessions.

Combining Private and Group Training

The most effective approach often combines both private and group training:

  • Group classes: For learning new techniques, rolling with different partners, and building endurance
  • Private lessons: For personalized instruction, addressing specific weaknesses, and accelerating progress
  • Open mat: For applying what you've learned in a less structured environment

Investment in Your Development

While private lessons represent an additional investment, they can significantly accelerate your progress and help you achieve your goals faster. Many students find that the time and money saved through faster improvement makes private lessons a worthwhile investment.
